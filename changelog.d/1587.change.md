`attrs.validators.instance_of()` now raises a clear `TypeError` at validator construction time when called with something that is not a class, a tuple of classes, or a `types.UnionType` of classes (e.g. the string `"int"` or the integer `1`). Previously the bad argument was accepted and only surfaced later as the cryptic `TypeError: isinstance() arg 2 must be a type, a tuple of types, or a union` at the first validation site, several frames away from where the validator was built.
