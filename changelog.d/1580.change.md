`lt`, `le`, `ge`, and `gt` now raise `ValueError` (with a clear, attribute-aware message) when called with a value that can't be compared to the bound, e.g. `attr.ib(validator=ge(5))` against `None`. Previously the underlying `TypeError` from `operator.<`/`>` propagated unchanged as `TypeError: '>=' not supported between instances of 'NoneType' and 'int'`, which doesn't name the attribute or the validator.
